FAQ

Can't I just ask ChatGPT to generate these files?

You can generate generic files. But the value isn't the files — it's the right guardrails for your specific repo: the correct test gates, the right forbidden zones, the actual CI commands that work with your stack, and the handoff that teaches you how to use it all. Generic templates break on real repos.

Do you need access to my production environment?

No. The audit is read-only on your codebase. I never touch production, databases, or secrets. All changes go through PRs that you review and merge.

What if my repo doesn't have tests or CI at all?

That's exactly who this is for. The audit will flag the gaps, and the sprint will set up the minimum viable safety net. You don't need 100% coverage — you need the critical paths covered and the gates in place so things don't get worse.

Does this fix bugs or add features?

No. This is strictly agent governance, CI safety, and repo hardening. No application feature work. That's a different engagement — and one that will go much better once your repo has proper agent guardrails.

What happens after the 48 hours?

You get the handoff call + a 7-day improvement plan. If you want ongoing support, the Monthly AgentOps Retainer keeps your governance up to date as your codebase grows. But you own all the files and can maintain them yourself.

I'm not using Windsurf — I use Cursor/Codex. Does this still work?

Yes. AGENTS.md is tool-agnostic governance. The WINDSURF_RULES.md can be adapted for Cursor (.cursorrules) or Codex. The CI gates, PR templates, and safety scripts work regardless of which agent you use.
